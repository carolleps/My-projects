Echidna

Echidnas are monotremes, or mammals that lay eggs.

  • Scientific Name: Tachyglossus Aculeatus
  • Average Lenght: 30-45 cm
  • Average weight: 7kg
  • Habitat: Forests and woodlands of Australia and New Guinea

Echidnas are medium-sized, solitary mammals covered with coarse hair and spines. They are found only in Australia and New Guinea. When confronted by predators, echidnas will lodge themselves under a rock or log only leaving the sharp spines visible. It moves around making snuffling noises while it hunts for food.
